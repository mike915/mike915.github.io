1. MacQuest --- On-campus indoor navigation systems

MacQuest is an APP developed by the Wireless System Research Group (WiSeR) from the Department of Computing and Software, McMaster University. It provides on-campus navigation and other campus-related services to visitors to McMaster University, McMaster students, staff and faculty. Its main features include but not limited to:

Comprehensive McMaster campus map and building floor plan visualization
- Outdoor: buildings, path ways, parking lots
- Indoor: rooms, bathrooms, elevators, stairs, and hallways
Building index
- Easily find a target building and display its location on the map
Navigation
- Any pairs between rooms, buildings, arbitrary on-campus locations
Hot spot search
- Nearest bathroom, stairs, elevators
Localization
- Mainly for outdoor
Next bus
- Check last, next and following bus arrival time near campus by bus stop and route number

MacQuest is also a research platform for location-based services and mobile participatory sensing. To better serve you, please help us improve the quality of maps and contribute location data by clicking the button "I am here" in the popup box of your current room or location.

3. Two other things could be mentioned

Translation of book "Mastering Algorithms with C"


The original thought of translation was to share the knowledge. “Mastering Algorithms in C” is the most readable algorithms book I've ever encountered. And I was surprised that, after 10 years of its publishing, there wasn’t a Chinese edition to read yet. As most of the Chinese programmer didn’t have the habit to read English technique book, and this is an awesome book, I decided to translate this book from English to Chinese with one of my friends – Colin Chen. It took us three months to translate the book and another month to revise the typos. Eventually, it was published in late 2012. This definitely was a unique experience I ever had. I’m so happy, exciting and proud of that.

Technique blogger --- GeekFan.net

There are four co-founders of this website (include me). The purpose of building this site is to translate and share the latest tech news and articles (ex. Raspberry Pie, Arduino, Linux, Android, iOS) to Chinese tech-fans. We collect articles from TechCrunch, Hacker News, howtogeek, gizmodo, lifehacker, makezine, instructables and so on, then sort out several most innovative and interesting ones, and translate them into Chinese, finally publish them on our website.

After I moved to Canada for a while, I stopped contributing to GeekFan.net and dropped off from the team, because I had a tight schedule of studies, and didn’t have enough time to handle all the works.

However, GeekFan leaves me lots of wonderful memories, so glad that I have been a part of it.
