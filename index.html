About work

Well, I don’t want to make this section becoming a resume page, however, if you really prefer something more resume-style, feel free to go to my LinkedIn website. Here, I would like to say something more casual and relaxed.

The first company I worked is an electronics manufacturing company which I stayed at for nearly two years. Our products are called “Customer Queue Systems”. Since every time I explained it to other people, they usually had no idea what is it. So I would like to put it in this way. Every time, when you go into a bank branch, the first thing you need to do is to get a ticket from a machine, then you could go to the waiting area for calling. After a while, when the bank teller pushes a button on the caller and meanwhile you see your number on the led display, you would be served. So the ticket machine, the callers and the led displays as a whole are named “Customer Queue Systems”.

My second company is a big telecommunication company which is excellent at researching and developing optical communication device. I worked on PTN products for about two years and spend another two years on developing IP RAN products. I know if you are not a telecommunication guy, you absolutely don’t know what I am saying. Yes, it’s true. I always believe communication is a special and important industry in the real world. Most of the popular technologies and electronic devices are based on the Internet, and the connections of the Internet is based on thousands or millions of telecommunication devices. To be a qualified communication software engineer, you need to be proficient in programming and communication protocol, and also are required to have some knowledge of hardware design.

I’m a person who always could and earn to learn a lot from work. However, at some spot of your career, you would feel that you have touched the ceiling of the job, you cannot absorb knowledge as much as you did before. If that happened, you can choose to leave and face the new challenge from the outside world. That's why I’m here.
